Spanish Empire Overview

  • Name: Osman I (Osman Gazi)

  • Founded: 1299, in Söğüt, Anatolia

  • Accomplishments: Unified Turkic tribes, launched conquests into Byzantine territory, established the longest-lasting Islamic empire in history

  • Cause of Death: Natural causes, circa 1326

  • Burial Location: Bursa, Turkey

Greatest Achievements

  • Conquest of Constantinople (1453) under Mehmed II
  • Control of three continents: Europe, Asia, Africa
  • Creation of elite Janissary corps and centralized bureaucracy
  • Architectural legacy: mosques, palaces, and fortresses
  • Naval dominance in the Mediterranean during the 16th century

Greatest Defeats

  • Siege of Vienna (1529, 1683)
  • Battle of Lepanto (1571)
  • Russo-Turkish Wars
  • Balkan Wars (1912–1913)
  • World War I and dissolution of the empire

Timeline

  • 1299 – Osman I founds the Ottoman Beylik
  • 1453 – Mehmed II conquers Constantinople
  • 1520–1566 – Reign of Suleiman the Magnificent
  • 1683 – Failed siege of Vienna
  • 1798 – Napoleon invades Egypt
  • 1878 – Loss of Balkan territories
  • 1914–1918 – World War I
  • 1922 – Abolition of the Sultanate
